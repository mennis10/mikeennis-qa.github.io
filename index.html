The Problem

Sound familiar?

These are the four symptoms that tell me an organization's QA program has a structural problem — not a people problem.

🔥

Defects keep escaping to production

Your team is testing, but bugs still reach customers. The root cause is almost always upstream — in how requirements are written and reviewed, not how tests are executed.

🤖

Automation investment isn't paying off

You've invested in automation tooling but the coverage is low, the maintenance burden is high, and you're still running most regression manually before every release.

📊

Leadership has no quality visibility

Your CIO asks for quality metrics and the answer is a spreadsheet. There's no dashboard, no trend data, and no way to make release decisions based on objective quality signals.

🐢

QA is still a phase gate, not embedded

Testing happens after development finishes. QA teams aren't in sprint planning or backlog refinement. Quality is a bottleneck rather than a built-in property of every sprint.
